2025 BA Winter Camp

By Hubert Huang 13 February 2025

Introduction

Just like last summer, I have returned to the BA Camp (officially named the Business Administration Camp; hereafter referred to as “BA”) to serve as a volunteer.

However, my role this year is quite different. I am primarily serving as a lecturer, sharing my experiences with high school students and introducing common misconceptions about the Business Administration Department, its courses, and career Paths.

Today, I will briefly share the content of my speech.

The Reason Behind Being a Lecturer

First and foremost, the primary goal of this camp is to help high school students gain a deeper understanding of the major through well-structured courses and activities. With this in mind, I believe that my current field of study can offer valuable insights to high school students.

Furthermore, I consider myself a cross-disciplinary learner, having explored areas such as management, economics, and computer science. Through my experiences in interdisciplinary learning, I hope to share the value and positioning of the Business Administration Department with high school students.

Lastly, as one of the most diverse fields of study, Department of Business Administration at National Taiwan University strongly encourages interdisciplinary learning and provides abundant resources for students to explore beyond their major. I believe that many learning experiences and stories from my peers and seniors are worth sharing with high school students.

Common Misconceptions

As a major with a unique positioning, the BA Department is often misunderstood in many ways. Here are two common misconceptions I’d like to share:

1. “BA students learn everything but master nothing”

Many people believe that the BA curriculum lacks specialization, as it touches on various subjects without providing in-depth training in any single field. To some extent, this is true. Compared to other majors with a highly specialized focus, the BA curriculum is more dispersed. If we look only at the course structure, the specialized training in a single domain may not be as intensive as in other fields.

However, this is precisely what makes the BA department valuable. Its breadth allows students to explore multiple disciplines and provides the flexibility to engage in interdisciplinary learning. For students with a clear academic or career plan, this flexibility reduces the burden of overly rigid coursework, enabling them to focus on their areas of interest and specialize accordingly.

2. “BA students are bad at math”

This is a common misconception.

In reality, management decisions cannot rely solely on intuition. Many critical decisions require mathematical and scientific analysis to reach optimal conclusions. “Management Science” is a discipline that applies mathematical methods to decision-making, demonstrating the essential role of quantitative analysis in business management.

Additionally, many BA graduates with strong mathematical skills continue their studies in fields like Industrial Engineering and Data Science, further debunking this myth.

Career Path

Many high school students worry about the career paths of BA graduates or, more precisely, are unsure of what career opportunities are available to them. So, I took some time to research data from Taiwanese human resource companies and National Taiwan University statistics, and I discovered the following trends:

1. Diverse Industries

The most common industry among BA alumni is “the internet industry”. Of course, the internet industry encompasses various fields, but this finding suggests that BA graduates are not solely concentrated in traditional business sectors like banking. Instead, they work across a wide range of industries.

2. Diverse Roles

BA alumni hold a wide variety of positions, ranging from materials project manager, supply chain manager, headhunter, and e-commerce marketer to data scientist and software engineer. This diversity highlights the flexibility of a BA degree and the broad range of career opportunities available to its graduates.

Summary

I am very grateful for the opportunity to serve as a lecturer and share my experiences and thoughts with high school students.

I hope this sharing session can benefit any student who finds it helpful. If I have the time and ability in the future, I would be more than happy to share my story with even more students.
